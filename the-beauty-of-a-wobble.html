The Beauty of a Wobble

Published on November 23, 2023

We are sold a vision of balance as a static, perfect state of equilibrium. We imagine it as the top of a mountain: once we arrive, we can plant our flag and enjoy the stable, serene view forever. Life, in this model, is a linear journey upward. The wobbles, the stumbles, the times we lose our footing are seen as failures, proof that we are not yet "balanced."

But after years of chasing this mirage, I've come to a different conclusion. This ideal of perfect, unwavering balance is a myth, and a dangerous one at that. It sets us up for a lifetime of self-recrimination. True balance is not a destination you arrive at. It is a dynamic process. It is the balance of a rider on a bicycle, a tightrope walker on a wire, a surfer on a wave. It is a continuous, subtle, and often imperceptible series of corrections.

A wobble is not a sign that you are failing at balance. The wobble is the process of balancing. It is the feedback loop of life. You lean too far into work, and a wobble of burnout or a strained relationship pulls you back. You lean too far into leisure, and a wobble of anxiety or boredom nudges you toward productivity. The goal is not to eliminate the wobbles, but to become more adept at feeling them, responding to them, and trusting your ability to find your center again.

The beauty lies in this dance of correction. It requires presence, awareness, and a light touch. It asks us to be less rigid and more fluid. When we expect a wobble-free life, every tremor becomes a crisis. When we accept wobbling as the natural state of things, we develop resilience and grace. We learn to ride the bumps instead of fighting them. We understand that the center is not a fixed point, but a path we are constantly tracing with our movements. The beauty is not in a static pose, but in the graceful, ongoing, and deeply human act of not falling over.
