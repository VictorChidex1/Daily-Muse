The Grace of a Good End

Published on November 11, 2023

Our culture is infatuated with beginnings. We celebrate the new year, the new job, the new project, the clean slate. We have endless advice on how to start strong. But we are remarkably illiterate when it comes to endings. We let projects fizzle out from lack of interest. We leave jobs with a muted email and a sense of relief. We allow relationships to decay into silence rather than giving them a dignified conclusion. An ending, to us, is often a failure, a defeat, or at best, an awkwardness to be rushed through.

But what if we are missing one of the most important arts of a well-lived life: the art of ending well? A good ending is not a passive cessation. It is an active, deliberate completion. It is the final, resonant chord in a symphony that gives meaning to all the movement that came before it. Without it, the music just stops, leaving the audience unsettled and confused.

A good ending provides closure. It allows us to gather the lessons learned, to sift the gold from the gravel of the experience. It is a process of integration, where we ask, "What did this chapter teach me? What am I grateful for? What do I need to release?" This act of conscious reflection prevents us from carrying the unresolved baggage of one phase into the next.

It is also an act of respect‚Äîfor our own time and effort, and for the other people involved. A proper goodbye at the end of a job honors the colleagues and the work you shared. A deliberate conclusion to a project, perhaps marked by a final review or a celebratory dinner, honors the energy invested. It draws a clear, clean line.

I've started practicing this by building "ending rituals" into my life. When I finish a major piece of writing, I don't just hit "send" and move on. I take a moment to read it one last time, to acknowledge the work, and to literally say, "This is complete." It sounds silly, but it works. It closes the psychic loop. By ending well, we honor the journey we've just completed and, paradoxically, we free up all our energy to truly begin the next one with a clean slate and an open heart.
