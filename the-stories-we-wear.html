The Stories We Wear

Published on November 8, 2023

We often think of our identity as a fixed, internal essence, a core self. But I've come to understand it as something more fluid and active: a collection of stories we tell ourselves, narratives we don like clothing every single day. These are not just casual thoughts; they are the lenses through which we interpret the world. The story of "I'm not a math person" becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy, shutting down curiosity the moment numbers are involved. The story of "I'm always lucky" primes you to see opportunity where others see chance.

These narratives are often handed to us in childhood, stitched together by early successes and failures, and reinforced by the people around us. We wear them for so long that we forget they are separate from our skin. We mistake the costume for the actor. The terrifying and liberating truth is that we are the tailors of these stories. We can, with conscious effort, try on a new outfit.

This isn't about positive thinking or self-deception. It's about conscious experimentation. What if, before a daunting challenge, you deliberately took off the heavy, familiar cloak of "I'm an imposter" and slipped on the lighter, more adventurous jacket of "I am a curious explorer"? How would your posture change? Your openness to feedback? Your resilience in the face of a setback?

I've done this. Before a difficult conversation, I've consciously chosen to wear the story of "listener" instead of "defender." When tackling a creative block, I've worn the story of "playful experimenter" instead of "critical judge." The external circumstances didn't change, but my internal posture did. And from that new posture, new actions emerged: more patience, more courage, more creativity. The story we wear changes our center of gravity, and from that new center, we move through the world differently. We are not changing our core self; we are discovering that our core self is far more vast and adaptable than any single story we've ever told about it.
